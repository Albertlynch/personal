Humble Beginnings:

All of this started during the summer break of 2020 when our dad suggested that we should go sell t-shirts at the Pier instead of playing video games. He had a few t-shirts that he had previously printed out, and our dad agreed to let us use his t-shirts. My brother, Isaac, who was 11 then, and I, just 9 years old, were intrigued but nervous. Our dad reassured us it would be fun and a great way to learn how to earn and manage our money.

Setting Up Shop on the Pier

San Diego’s pier was the perfect setting for our little stand. With its steady flow of tourists, locals, and ocean breeze, it felt alive with opportunity. My brother and I set up our small table, and we intricately arranged the t-shirts we had previously folded on the surface. I, being the younger but more confident one, handled most of the talking. Isaac stayed close, learning from me and occasionally helping out. Our dad stood nearby, offering advice and encouragement but letting us take the lead. We quickly noticed that people were not only interested in our shirts but also impressed by our ages. Many customers gave us extra tips and kind words, which gave my brother and me more confidence.

Lessons Learned and Success Earned

We did surprisingly well for our first time, but it wasn’t just about making money. It was about understanding the value of hard work and learning how to interact with people. Of course, there were challenges. Sometimes, a shirt didn’t fit someone quite right, or a potential customer would walk away after asking about prices. But we learned to adapt and stay positive, focusing on the people who did stop and support us. By the end of the summer, we had made enough money to treat ourselves to a few things we’d been wanting. Most importantly, though, we learned how to make money and made these amazing memories.
